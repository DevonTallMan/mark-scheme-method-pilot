📖 STATION 01 — INTEL BRIEF
Theory Reading: The DIK Hierarchy

Read carefully — this is the foundation. The examiner rewards students who can clearly distinguish between all three levels.

📡WHAT IS DATA?

Data is raw, unprocessed facts and figures. On its own, data has no meaning. It is simply a collection of numbers, characters, or symbols that have not yet been interpreted.

Example: 102.4, 98.6, 104.1, 99.8 — these are temperature readings. Without context, they tell us nothing about what is happening or why.

Examiner Note: Data is always described as "raw" and "without context". If your answer says "data is useful to businesses", you have missed the point — it is not useful until it becomes information.
PROPERTYDESCRIPTION
RawUnprocessed. Not analysed or given context.
Meaningless aloneCannot be acted upon without processing.
ObjectiveJust facts — no interpretation applied.
📊WHAT IS INFORMATION?

Information is data that has been processed, organised, and given context so that it becomes meaningful. Information answers the question: what is happening?

Example: "Server temperature reading of 104.1°F — the server is overheating." The raw number (data) has been compared against a threshold and given meaning (information).

Key Process: Data → Information requires processing. This includes sorting, filtering, comparing, summarising, or applying a rule. The context is what transforms data into information.
PROPERTYDESCRIPTION
ProcessedData has been organised, compared, or contextualised.
MeaningfulHas context. Answers "what is happening?"
Actionable basisEnables decisions — but is not the decision itself.
🧠WHAT IS KNOWLEDGE?

Knowledge is information combined with experience and understanding to enable decision-making. Knowledge answers: what should we do?

Example: "Server temperature has exceeded safe threshold — shut down the server immediately to prevent hardware damage and data loss." This is a decision informed by past experience, protocols, and the information at hand.

The Mark You're Missing: The Impact mark (3rd mark) in exam questions almost always requires knowledge-level thinking — the business consequence or decision triggered by the information. "This allows the organisation to..." is how you signal knowledge.
PROPERTYDESCRIPTION
Experience-basedCombines information with prior understanding.
Decision-enablingAnswers "what should we do?" — the action.
Business ImpactDescribes the commercial or operational outcome.
