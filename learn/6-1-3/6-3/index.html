⬡ STATIONS 01–06 — DATA SYSTEMS

Validation, Wrangling, GIGO, BIS, CRM & ERDs

Clean data is the foundation of every reliable system. Examiners test whether you know the difference.

✅ VALIDATION
Checks data is the right format
Automated — done by the system
e.g. "Is this a valid email address?"
Range check, format check, presence check
Can catch: wrong type, out of range, missing
VS
🔍 VERIFICATION
Checks data is accurate and correct
Often requires human checking
e.g. "Is this the customer's real email?"
Double-entry, proofreading, comparison
Cannot catch: correct format but wrong data
CONCEPT 01
✅
Data Validation
Automated checks that ensure data entered into a system meets predefined rules before it is accepted. Common types: range check, format check, presence check, type check, length check.
EXAM EXAMPLE
A form rejects a date of birth entered as "32/13/1999" — a format and range check fires before the data is stored.
CONCEPT 02
🔧
Data Wrangling
The process of cleaning, transforming, and restructuring raw data into a usable format for analysis. Involves removing duplicates, filling missing values, standardising formats, and correcting errors.
EXAM EXAMPLE
A data analyst converts 50 CSV files with inconsistent date formats (DD/MM vs MM/DD) into a single standardised dataset before running sales analysis.
CONCEPT 03
🗑️
GIGO
Garbage In, Garbage Out. If poor-quality data enters a system, the outputs — reports, decisions, predictions — will also be poor quality, regardless of how sophisticated the system is.
EXAM EXAMPLE
A stock prediction algorithm trained on incorrectly labelled sales data produces inaccurate forecasts, leading to £2M of excess inventory. GIGO in action.
CONCEPT 04
📊
Business Information Systems (BIS)
Integrated systems that collect, process, store, and distribute data to support organisational decision-making. Examples include ERP, MIS, DSS. BIS turns raw operational data into management intelligence.
EXAM EXAMPLE
A Management Information System (MIS) aggregates daily sales data from all 200 branches and generates a weekly executive dashboard — enabling strategic pricing decisions.
CONCEPT 05
👥
CRM Systems
Customer Relationship Management. Systems that store and manage all customer data — purchase history, interactions, preferences — to improve retention, personalisation, and sales targeting.
EXAM EXAMPLE
A CRM flags a high-value customer who hasn't purchased in 90 days, triggering an automated personalised offer — reducing churn and protecting revenue.
CONCEPT 06
🗂️
Entity Relationship Diagrams (ERDs)
Visual models showing how data entities (e.g. Customer, Order, Product) relate to each other in a database. ERDs define relationships (one-to-one, one-to-many) and are used to design relational databases.
EXAM EXAMPLE
An ERD for an e-commerce system shows: one Customer can place many Orders; one Order can contain many Products (many-to-many via an Order_Item junction table).

FLIP TO REVEAL
What is the key difference between validation and verification?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Validation checks data is the right format (automated). Verification checks data is actually correct (often human-led). A valid email format ≠ a real email address.
FLIP TO REVEAL
A customer enters their age as "250". Which validation check catches this?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Range check — the value 250 falls outside the acceptable range (e.g. 0–120) and is rejected before being stored in the system.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Explain GIGO and give a business consequence.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Garbage In, Garbage Out — if inaccurate data enters a system, outputs will also be inaccurate. A retailer using incorrectly logged sales data would produce flawed demand forecasts, leading to stock shortages or excess.
FLIP TO REVEAL
What business problem does a CRM system solve?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
CRM centralises all customer data — purchase history, preferences, interactions — enabling personalised marketing, proactive churn prevention, and improved customer lifetime value.
FLIP TO REVEAL
In an ERD, what does a "one-to-many" relationship mean?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
One record in entity A can be linked to many records in entity B, but not vice versa. Example: one Customer can place many Orders, but each Order belongs to exactly one Customer.
FLIP TO REVEAL
What is data wrangling and why is it necessary before analysis?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Wrangling cleans and transforms raw data into a consistent, usable format. Without it, inconsistencies (duplicate records, mixed date formats, null values) corrupt analysis and violate GIGO principles.
