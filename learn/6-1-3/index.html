⬡ STATIONS 01–06 — HOW DATA IS GENERATED

D2D, H2D & Machine/Sensor Sources

Understand where data comes from — and how to explain it for full marks.

SOURCE TYPE 1
📡
Device-to-Device (D2D)
Data generated automatically when one device communicates with another — no human involvement. D2D is the backbone of IoT, automated logistics, and smart infrastructure.
REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES
A delivery van's GPS unit automatically transmits location data to the depot server every 30 seconds. A smart thermostat sends temperature readings to a cloud server. RFID tags on products update stock systems as they move through a warehouse.
IoTAutomatedNo human inputReal-time
SOURCE TYPE 2
👤
Human-to-Device (H2D)
Data created when a human interacts with a device — typing, clicking, swiping, submitting forms. Every deliberate action a person takes with technology generates H2D data.
REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES
A customer completing a checkout form on an e-commerce site. A student typing an answer into a revision platform. A doctor entering a diagnosis into a hospital system. A user clicking "Like" on a social media post.
User inputIntentionalForms & clicksBehavioural
SOURCE TYPE 3
⚙️
Machine & Sensor Sources
Data generated by physical sensors, machinery, and automated systems monitoring the environment. Sensors capture data that humans cannot consistently or accurately measure manually.
REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES
Temperature sensors in a food cold-chain. Vibration sensors on industrial machinery detecting wear. Air quality monitors in smart cities. Motion sensors in a retail store counting footfall. Biometric readers at building access points.
Physical worldContinuousEnvironmentalHigh volume

FLIP TO REVEAL
What is the key difference between D2D and H2D data generation?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
D2D requires no human involvement — devices communicate automatically. H2D is generated through deliberate human actions such as typing, clicking, or submitting data.
FLIP TO REVEAL
A warehouse uses RFID tags on every pallet. When a pallet passes a scanner, stock records update automatically. What type is this?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
D2D — The RFID tag (device) communicates with the scanner (device) without any human action. The stock update is fully automated.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Why do sensor sources generate data that humans cannot replicate manually?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Sensors can monitor continuously, at microsecond intervals, in hostile environments, and with far greater precision than any human observer — making them essential for real-time monitoring.
FLIP TO REVEAL
A customer fills in a returns form on a retailer's website. What data generation type is this, and what business value does it create?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
H2D. Business value: return reason data allows identification of product quality issues, enabling the business to reduce future defect rates and protect profit margins.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Give one risk to a business if its sensor data is inaccurate or delayed.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Decisions based on faulty data lead to operational failure — e.g. a temperature sensor malfunction in cold storage could result in stock spoilage and significant financial loss.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Name three different data sources a modern supermarket uses and classify each.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
D2D: RFID stock tags → warehouse system. H2D: Loyalty card swipe at checkout. Sensor: Temperature sensors in refrigeration units. Each creates different but complementary data streams.
