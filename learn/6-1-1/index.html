⬡ STATION 01 — THEORY + PRACTICE

Data, Information & Knowledge

Master the hierarchy. Then prove it.

TIER 1 — RAW
📊
Data
Raw, unprocessed facts and figures with no context or meaning. Data alone cannot inform a decision — it is simply numbers, symbols, or characters.
EXAMPLE
102.4 // 847ms // 3,200 // TRUE
TIER 2 — PROCESSED
📋
Information
Data that has been processed, organised, or structured so it becomes meaningful. Information provides context and answers the question: "What does this mean?"
EXAMPLE
Server temperature is 102.4°F — above safe operating range
TIER 3 — ACTIONABLE
🧠
Knowledge
Information combined with experience and insight that drives action. Knowledge answers: "What should we do about this?" — this is the Impact mark.
EXAMPLE
Shut down the server immediately to prevent hardware failure and data loss
FLIP TO REVEAL
What is the key difference between Data and Information?
ANSWER
Data is raw and unprocessed. Information is data that has been given context and meaning through processing.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Why does the examiner reward "Knowledge" answers more than "Information" answers?
ANSWER
Knowledge demonstrates the Impact — it shows how data drives business decisions and creates real value. This earns the 3rd mark.
FLIP TO REVEAL
"Sales dropped 23% in Q3." What tier is this?
ANSWER
Information — it's processed data with context, but doesn't yet specify what action to take.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Give an example of Knowledge from a retail business context.
ANSWER
Knowing that umbrella sales spike 340% before Bank Holidays allows the business to pre-stock and increase revenue.
