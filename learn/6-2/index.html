⬡ STATIONS 01–06 — WHY ORGANISATIONS NEED DATA

Operational, Tactical & Strategic Value

Master the three tiers. Then prove it in exam conditions.

TIER 1 — DAY-TO-DAY
⚙️
Operational Data
Used for immediate, day-to-day decisions and processes. Operational data keeps the business running right now — stock levels, transactions, staff rotas, delivery tracking.
BUSINESS EXAMPLE
A supermarket checks real-time stock data to trigger automatic reorders before shelves empty.
TIER 2 — MEDIUM-TERM
🧭
Tactical Data
Supports decisions over weeks or months. Tactical data helps managers allocate resources, adjust campaigns, and respond to patterns spotted in operational data.
BUSINESS EXAMPLE
A retailer analyses 3 months of sales data to reallocate marketing spend from underperforming channels to social media.
TIER 3 — LONG-TERM
🏛️
Strategic Data
Informs executive decisions about the organisation's future direction — market entry, acquisitions, product lines. Strategic data drives decisions years in advance.
BUSINESS EXAMPLE
A logistics firm analyses 5-year freight volume trends to decide whether to expand into Eastern European markets.
🎯
◈ HOW THIS EARNS MARKS — THE NEI CONNECTION
An examiner question like "Explain how a business uses data to add value" wants all three components: N Name the tier/use (e.g. "tactical data analysis"), E Explain the mechanism (how the data is used), I State the impact (the measurable business outcome). Students who stop at [E] miss the third mark every time.

FLIP TO REVEAL
What distinguishes operational data from strategic data?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Operational data drives immediate, day-to-day decisions (hours/days). Strategic data informs long-term direction (months/years). Timescale and scope are the key differences.
FLIP TO REVEAL
A marketing manager uses last quarter's CTR data to reallocate the ad budget. What tier is this?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Tactical — it spans multiple weeks, involves resource allocation, and is managed at middle-management level rather than front-line or boardroom.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Give one reason why a business without data is at a competitive disadvantage.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Without data, decisions are based on intuition rather than evidence — leading to inefficient resource allocation, missed market opportunities, and inability to detect risk in advance.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Why does the examiner award an Impact mark for strategic data questions?
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Because strategic data directly affects long-term business value — market position, profitability, and competitive advantage. The examiner wants you to connect data use to a business outcome.
FLIP TO REVEAL
What is a "data-driven decision"? Give a 3-tier example.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Op: Reorder stock (live sensor data). Tac: Reallocate marketing (quarterly results). Str: Enter new market (5-year trend analysis). All three use data to reduce risk and increase certainty.
FLIP TO REVEAL
Name two business functions that rely on operational data and explain why.
↕ tap to flip
ANSWER
Logistics — requires real-time delivery tracking to manage routes. Customer service — needs live order status data to resolve queries instantly. Both demand up-to-the-minute accuracy.
